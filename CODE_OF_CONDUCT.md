# Code of Conduct

## The short version

Treat others with respect. No harassment, discrimination, or personal attacks. Keep discussions focused on the technical topic at hand.

## The longer version

This project is a small spare-time effort. There's no company behind it, no community manager, no enforcement team. What there is, is one maintainer trying to make a useful tool and occasionally interact with others who want to help or use it.

That means the bar for interaction is simple but non-negotiable:

- Be civil. People come from different backgrounds, languages, and experience levels.
- Assume good intent. Most misunderstandings are just that.
- Criticise ideas, not people. "This approach won't work because X" is fine. "You're clueless" is not.
- Respect the maintainer's decisions about project scope. If you disagree with a direction, explain why, but accept that the final call belongs to the person doing the work.
- Don't use the issue tracker or pull requests for unrelated advocacy.

## What happens when someone crosses the line

I'll delete or lock threads, edit or remove comments, and block accounts as needed. I won't spend a lot of time litigating who was at fault. If you feel wronged by a moderation action, open an issue with a short, factual explanation and we can talk about it.

## Reaching out privately

For anything that shouldn't be public — security issues, a serious concern about another contributor, anything else that benefits from a quiet conversation — email me or open a private advisory through GitHub. I'll respond when I can, keeping in mind this is not my day job.

## Scope

This code of conduct applies to the issue tracker, pull requests, discussions, and any other project-hosted space. Behaviour elsewhere is not my concern unless it spills into project spaces.
